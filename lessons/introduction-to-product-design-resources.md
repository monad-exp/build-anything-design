# Resources

- [Nielsen Norman Group: Product & UX Glossary](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/product-ux-glossary/) - key product design terms, including MVP, jobs to be done, and hypothesis-driven development
- [SVPG: Product vs Design](https://svpg.com/articles/product-vs-design/) - how product thinking and design work differ, useful for builders working on a team
- [Nielsen Norman Group: Problem Statements](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/problem-statements/) - how to frame user problems before jumping to feature ideas
- [Nielsen Norman Group: Outcomes vs Features](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/outcomes-vs-features/) - why teams should focus on outcomes and define success criteria early
- [Nielsen Norman Group: Personas vs Jobs-to-Be-Done](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/personas-jobs-be-done/) - how jobs-to-be-done thinking captures what users are trying to accomplish
- [Nielsen Norman Group: Unbridged Knowledge Gaps](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/unbridged-knowledge-gaps/) - why missing context hurts users and how teams assume too much shared knowledge
- [Nielsen Norman Group: Technical Jargon](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/technical-jargon/) - why insider language confuses users and how to write for people new to a product
- [Nielsen Norman Group: Translating UX Goals into Analytics Measurement Plans](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ux-goals-analytics/) - how to define success metrics tied to real user and business goals
- [Nielsen Norman Group: Minimum Viable Product (MVP)](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/mvp-definition/) - what an MVP is and how it helps teams learn before committing to a full build
- [Nielsen Norman Group: CSD Matrix](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/csd-matrix/) - a framework for certainties, suppositions, and doubts when testing product assumptions
- [Nielsen Norman Group: The Discovery Phase](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/discovery-phase/) - research approaches for exploring problems before scaling a build
- [Nielsen Norman Group: Usability Testing 101](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/usability-testing-101/) - how to validate whether users can complete important tasks
- [Nielsen Norman Group: Aesthetic-Usability Effect](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/aesthetic-usability-effect/) - why polished designs can feel easier to use even when core usability problems remain
